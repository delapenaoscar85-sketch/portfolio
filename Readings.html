PROFESSIONAL READINGS

PHILOSOPHY

Guiding with Empathy, Empowering through Independence As a future molder of young minds. I believe that education is most effective when it places the learner at the center.

As an intern, my goal is to create an environment where students are active participants in their own learning. I will encourage them to think critically, explore their interests, and take responsibility for their growth. I see myself not just as a fountain of knowledge, but as a facilitator who guides them to discover their full potential on their own steps.

However, I also recognize that there are times when structure and direction are necessary. Thus, that time will be the right moment where I embrace a teacher-centered approach when the need arises. When students face difficulties, when concepts are complex, or when order and discipline are required, I will step forward to provide clear instructions, strong leadership, and direct support.

Ultimately, my philosophy is about balance: I will step back to let students shine and learn independently, but I will step up to lead and guide them whenever they need help, as I believe that every student can learn but not in the same day and not in the same way, every learner has their own time to shine. Just like flowers that bloom depending on their timeframe, when effective pedagogy applied.

LAWS

Learning about the educational laws in the Philippines made me realize that these are not just complicated words written in books or documents they are promises and protections for every student and teacher.

As a future teacher, I also understood that these laws are my guide. They tell me what is right and what is best for my students. They remind me that teaching is not just about teaching subjects, but also about protecting the dignity and welfare of the learners under my care.

Of course, I know that having laws is one thing and making them work perfectly in every school is another challenge. There are still things that need to be improved. But simply knowing that we have these laws gives me hope. It means our country truly cares about the youth.

In the end, these laws are like the strong roots of a tree. They hold the education system steady so that every student just like flowers can have the opportunity to bloom beautifully and safely in their own time.

THEORIES

Exploring different educational theories like Essentialism and Constructivism made me realize that there is no single "perfect way" to teach. Instead, being a good teacher means knowing when and how to use the right approach.

On the other hand, Constructivism showed me the beauty of letting students discover things on their own. It believes that learners are not empty vessels to be filled, but rather active creators of their own knowledge. This is where my student-centered philosophy comes in. Just like flowers, you cannot force them to open; you just provide the sunlight and water, and they will bloom in their own way. Constructivism allows students to think critically, explore, and build their own understanding.

I also realized that other theories play important roles too. Some students need positive reinforcement, while others learn best by observing or interacting with others.

Putting it all together, I learned that I don't have to stick to just one theory. I can be an Essentialist when I need to teach important values and basic skills, and I can be a Constructivist when I want them to think deeply and learn independently.

In the end, these theories are just tools. My goal is to use them wisely so that every student can learn, grow, and succeed in the way that fits them best.
